My Story

The True Origin Story of GUBER & Gubee

This isn’t a startup pitch. It’s where I really came from, what I survived, and why GUBER exists at all.

GUBER didn’t come from a business plan or some tech meeting. It came from my life — the real, messy parts that forced me to rebuild myself from the ground up.

Back in 2016, I built my own business from nothing. I was grinding, hustling, trying to create stability with my own hands. Then the woman I loved started medical school, and I did what I believed a supportive man should do — I tried to hold everything together for both of us.

For over a year, I drove four hours back and forth between cities, trying to make money while still being present for her. I didn’t shut my business down immediately. I fought for it. I stretched myself thin. Eventually the constant travel wore me down, and I started selling off the things I owned — tools, equipment, whatever I had — just to survive and keep supporting her dreams.

Years later, when her residency ended, something similar happened again… except this time, I wasn’t juggling two worlds. I was just waiting.

She told me she was “back home with her mom,” waiting on military orders. She cried to me, said she felt like a failure, said she didn’t want to burden me because she wouldn’t have income for a while. And I believed her. I prepared myself for when she’d call and say it was time for us to move forward.

But what I later came to understand was completely different from what I believed.

While she was calling me with tears and telling me she was at home waiting on orders, she had already begun a new chapter of her life — five states away — building a new career I knew nothing about. From my perspective, everyone around her seemed aware. I was the only one left in the dark.

And that wasn’t all. For three months, I waited — believing the story I was being told. Believing she was sitting in her mother’s house, unsure of her future, struggling emotionally. But later, I learned she had already been established in that new place for months — working, settled in, building new friendships, possibly even new connections beyond that. From my point of view, she had already moved forward long before I realized something was wrong.

And she never told me. She let me wait. She let me hope. She let me hold on to a future she had already stepped out of.

When I finally dug deep and uncovered the truth myself — when I confronted her — I never heard from her again. No explanation. No honesty. No apology. Just silence.

One day, I was someone she cried to… and the next day, it felt like I never existed.

But the hardest part wasn’t the distance or the silence. It was the time I lost — time spent following her moves, time I could’ve used rebuilding myself, and time away from my mother… time I will never get back.

When my mom passed — the woman I always dreamed of blessing with a home — the person I loved didn’t show up. She didn’t stand beside me. She didn’t even help watch the dogs. I faced everything alone: the grief, the bills, the heartbreak, the confusion, and the reality that the life I believed in no longer existed.

The emotional weight became too heavy, and eventually I had to let our dogs go. Losing them felt like losing the last pieces of the future I thought I was building.

Sitting in that pain, I asked the question every man asks when he’s given everything he has:

“I guess I just wasn’t good enough… right?”

But that moment wasn’t the end of my story — it was the spark. I’ve survived things most people never witness. I’ve rebuilt from nothing more than once. And climbing out of the darkest season of my life, something clicked:

Most people don’t fall because they’re weak. They fall because help takes too long. Because opportunity moves too slow. Because life doesn’t wait for callbacks, approval, or a second chance.

Nobody should have to sit alone in a city waiting for life to give them a break. Nobody should lose everything because they couldn’t work today.

And that’s where GUBER came from. From real loss. From real survival. From rebuilding after heartbreak, confusion, and abandonment. From understanding what it feels like to need help right now and not have any.

GUBER isn’t just an app. It’s a second chance… and the beginning of something bigger.

GUBEE / GUBERMAN

The Origin of Gubee

Gubee didn’t come from marketing. He didn’t come from a design meeting. He came from the same place GUBER came from — me, my imagination, and the pain I had to grow through.

As a child, imagination was the only escape I had. When life felt heavy, my mind created worlds that kept me alive. I never grew out of that. Imagination was the one thing life could never take from me.

And after everything collapsed — the heartbreak, the lies, the silence, the funeral, the distance — that imagination returned with a new purpose.

Gubee became the child of my imagination. The identity I never got to raise. The future that was abandoned before it began. The symbol of everything I still had left inside me.

While adults see GUBER as opportunity, kids will see GUBEE as hope — a superhero of responsibility, ambition, work ethic, and a better future.

Gubee represents everything I never had growing up: someone to look up to, someone to inspire me, someone who made doing the right thing feel powerful and rewarding.

He represents the child whose future was walked away from… but now belongs to the world.

Gubee is not just a mascot. He’s purpose. He’s legacy. He’s the imagination that saved me — reborn to inspire millions.

This isn’t just my story. This is my why. Everything that broke me… built GUBER. Everything I lost… created Gubee. Everything I survived… became the foundation of a platform that will change lives.
